Introduction

The skin carries a
personal microbial signature

Discriminating >80% Different Two hands can differ by over 80% in the microbes they carry.
Recurring Persist Patterns hold Communities drift over time, yet recurring patterns stay recognisable.
Transferable Trace Left on touch Locard's principle — every contact leaves a trace — holds for microbes.
The core idea

Skin microbes carry informative signatures, transferred onto everything we touch. These signatures vary over time — but machine learning extracts the recurring, discriminatory patterns that reveal who was there, even where human DNA fails.

Metcalf et al., Trends in Biotechnology (2017) · Dass, Abbai & Ghai, Int. J. Legal Medicine (2026)02 / 23

Microbes That Remember YouMethods
Methods

Our end-to-end machine-learning pipeline

1
Load & joinGenus table + metadata on sample_id.
2
Prevalence filterKeep taxa in ≥10% of samples (1,176 → 679).
3
CLR transformCompositional log-ratio scaling.
4
Split by timepointTrain visits 1–2 · test on a later visit.
5
Five classifiersLR · SVM · RF · GB · k-NN.
Two learning modes, together

Supervised (LR · SVM · RF · GB · k-NN) → classify and identify who a sample came from.

Unsupervised (PCA · PCoA · k-means) → discover structure with no labels; here it is body-site, not person.

Dataset

Oh et al. 2016 (Cell) shotgun metagenomics — 585 samples · 12 individuals · 17 body sites · 1,176 genera per sample.

What drives the main axis — real skin biology, not artifact

Standardizing rescales all 679 genera to equal variance, amplifying rare soil taxa into PC1. On raw CLR the same axis is built from abundant skin residents — Cutibacterium, Malassezia, oral genera.

In simple terms: our method correctly picks up real skin bacteria, not background noise.

Which taxa actually identify a person

Each column is one person; each row a skin microbe. Red = more than the group average, blue = less — the number is that person's mean % abundance.

Microbes That Remember YouChallenges
Challenges

Hurdles to clear before court-ready evidence

Low-biomass samples Touch traces hold tiny amounts of DNA — strict contamination controls are essential.
The microbiome drifts Skin communities shift with time and season, so samples must be collected close together.
Lifestyle confounders Diet, hygiene products and cohabitation can blur the personal signal.
No local reference data Models only know what they're trained on — a UAE database must be built from scratch.
Court-ready standards A validated protocol with a known error rate is needed for admissibility (Frye / Daubert).
Cost, skills & diversity Sequencing, trained staff and the UAE's mobile population all raise the bar.
Hurdles drawn from forensic skin-microbiome literature — Metcalf 2017 · Dass et al. 2026 · Lei et al. 2024 · Clarke et al. 202218 / 23

Microbes That Remember YouConclusions
Conclusions

What we established, and what comes next

1
Individuals are identifiable
Genus-level skin microbiome hits 92.6% Top-1 ID on a hard future-visit split — real personal signal, not chance.
2
The signal is specific & defensible
Identity is carried by Corynebacterium, Cutibacterium, Malassezia and kin — matching published forensic markers, confirmed by our B/W test.
3
Site and population matter
ID works best at sebaceous sites; models don't transfer across populations — the Egypt gap is real and survives bootstrap.
4
The real deliverable is local data
Next: strain-level π on shotgun data (as Schmedes did) and a purpose-built UAE / Gulf reference database.
